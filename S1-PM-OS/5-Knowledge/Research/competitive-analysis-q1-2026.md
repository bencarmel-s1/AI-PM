# Competitive Analysis — Q1 2026

## Overview

This analysis covers Pulse's key competitors in the product analytics space. Focus: onboarding, session replay, and integrations — the three areas most relevant to Q2 priorities.

---

## Competitor Landscape

| Competitor | Positioning | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|------------|
| **Mixpanel** | Event analytics for product teams | Deep funnel/cohort analysis; strong data model | No session replay; complex setup |
| **FullStory** | Session replay + digital experience | Best-in-class session replay; enterprise features | Limited event analytics; expensive |
| **Heap** | Autocapture analytics | Low implementation friction (no SDK config needed) | Limited replay; less customizable |
| **PostHog** | Open source product analytics | Free tier; developer-first; session replay included | UX is rough; limited enterprise support |
| **Amplitude** | Enterprise product analytics | Deep analytics suite; enterprise integrations | Complex; slow to set up; expensive |

---

## Where Pulse Wins

1. **Qualitative + quantitative in one product** — Pulse is the only mid-market player combining funnel analytics AND session replay without paying for two separate products. FullStory has better replay, but no funnel. Mixpanel has better analytics, but no replay.

2. **Speed to value** — Our 4-hour median setup time beats the industry average (Heap is comparable due to autocapture; others are slower).

3. **Team-friendly** — Dashboard sharing, annotation layer, and Slack digest are differentiators that competitors underinvest in.

---

## Where We're Vulnerable

1. **Onboarding** — Heap's autocapture model means they have almost zero setup friction. Our current 11-day time-to-first-value is a risk in competitive deals where customers trial multiple tools.

2. **Integration breadth** — Amplitude and Mixpanel each have 80+ native integrations. We have 40. Shopify and Salesforce gaps are active deal-blockers (confirmed by Sales Q1 survey).

3. **Mobile** — PostHog and FullStory both have mobile session replay. Our mobile gap is a risk in competitive evaluations with mobile-first customers.

---

## Competitive Moves to Watch

- **Mixpanel (Jan 2026):** Launched a "no-code" onboarding flow builder. Reduces their setup friction. Directionally threatening to our onboarding advantage.
- **PostHog (Feb 2026):** Launched PostHog Cloud with EU data residency — unlocking European enterprise. Not a current risk for us but watch for FY27.
- **Heap (Mar 2026):** Acquired by Contentsquare. Unclear product direction post-acquisition. Could be a displacement opportunity if Heap customers get nervous.

---

## Implications for Q2 Roadmap

| Area | Competitive Implication |
|------|------------------------|
| Onboarding Revamp | Urgent — Mixpanel's no-code builder raises the bar. Need May 30 launch to stay competitive. |
| Alerts adoption | Low competitive pressure — most competitors don't have proactive alerting at our price point. |
| Integration Catalog v2 | Shopify + Salesforce are deal-blockers vs. Amplitude and Mixpanel. Q3 timing is risky. |
| Mobile session replay | FullStory and PostHog both have this. Growing gap, especially for mobile-first customers. |
