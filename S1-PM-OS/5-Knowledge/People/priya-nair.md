# Priya Nair

## Quick Facts
- Leads design for the Pulse product surface (joined Acme Analytics 2 years ago)
- Background in UX research before moving into product design
- Runs a small design team: 2 product designers + 1 UX researcher (shared)
- Very customer-oriented; insists on user validation before finalizing designs

---

## Communication Style

**Prefers:**
- Being brought in early — she hates designing to a locked spec
- Real user quotes and research signals, not "we think users want X"
- Slack + Figma comments for async feedback; async-first
- Clear design review timelines (she plans her own queue carefully)

**Dislikes:**
- Last-minute design requests ("we need this by Friday")
- Specs that dictate UI solutions instead of user problems
- Feedback that mixes aesthetic preferences with UX concerns — be specific

---

## Processing Style
Priya thinks in user journeys and mental models. She often reframes feature requests as "what is the user trying to accomplish?" before proposing a design. She'll push back on solutions she thinks don't serve the user — even if they come from senior stakeholders.

She's detail-oriented in Figma — her handoffs are thorough, but she expects engineers to flag edge cases back to her rather than make assumptions.

---

## How to Work Well with Priya
- Share user research findings and quotes when kicking off new work
- Give her the problem space + constraints, not the solution
- Review designs in Figma, not in meetings (she shares Figma links, not slide decks)
- For new initiatives, schedule a 30-min "problem framing" session before design starts

---

## What Priya Cares About
- Users — she'll always advocate for user needs over business convenience
- Design system consistency — she maintains the Acme design system and gets frustrated when engineers deviate from it
- Collaboration with research — she tries to loop in the UX researcher early; support this
