# Product Strategy Workflow

A complete system for developing defensible product strategies using AI as a thinking partner — not a decision-maker.

## When to Trigger

Say "product strategy", "develop a strategy", or "strategy for [product area]" when leadership has asked a strategic question or you need to set direction for a product area.

## What This Produces

- A competitive landscape summary (researched in parallel by AI agents)
- 5 strategic choices made and pressure-tested with devil's advocate
- A complete strategy document (Diagnosis + Guiding Policy + Coherent Actions)
- An executive presentation outline ready for Google Slides or PowerPoint

## Key Constraints

- Use Rumelt's Strategy Kernel as the primary framework — diagnosis → guiding policy → coherent actions
- Never jump from diagnosis straight to a feature list — the guiding policy is the strategy
- Every strategic choice must pass the devil's advocate test before moving on
- The test: "Would a reasonable person disagree?" — if not, it's too generic
- Strategy means saying NO to things — if everything is a priority, nothing is

## Which Framework to Use

- Developing strategy from scratch → Rumelt's Strategy Kernel
- Auditing an existing strategy → SWOT Analysis
- Pressure-testing strategic choices → Gibson-Biddle DHM
- All three together → SWOT for situation, Rumelt for strategy, DHM to pressure-test

## The 3-Phase Process

- Phase 1 — Diagnose: Research competitive landscape, identify the real challenge
- Phase 2 — Make Choices: 5 strategic decisions with devil's advocate pressure-testing
- Phase 3 — Synthesize: Complete strategy doc + executive slide deck
